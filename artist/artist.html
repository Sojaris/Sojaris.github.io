Swedish rapper, singer and songwriter

Yung Lean

Jonatan Aron Leandoer Håstad (born 18 July 1996) known professionally as Yung Lean .

Yung Lean rose to prominence in 2013 with his song "Ginseng Strip 2002", which went viral on YouTube.

Career

  • 2012–2013: Career beginnings, Lavender and Unknown Death 2002
  • 2014–2016: Unknown Memory and Warlord
  • 2017–2019: Stranger, Poison Ivy and Nectar
  • 2020–present: Starz, Blodhundar & Lullabies, In My Head, Stardust, and Sugar World

Artistry

XXL included him in their 2014 list of "15 European Rappers You Should Know".

Fact published another article titled "Rise of the Sad Boys: from Kompakt to Yung Lean, a history of how electronic musicians have worn their sadness on their sleeves".

Yung Lean was described by Entertainment Weekly as "loosely affiliated" with the cloud rap movement.

Yung Lean is also the vocalist for the post-punk band Död Mark.

Personal life

Outside of music, Håstad is writing a screenplay about a "strange gangster drama" and a rock opera about John Ausonius, a Swedish serial killer.Håstad has aspirations to become an actor.

Håstad has several hobbies, including reading, ceramics, painting, writing and playing piano.

Recognition

Håstad has been credited for being a pioneer in the emo rap, cloud rap, and SoundCloud rap genres. In 2019, Yung Lean was awarded the Bram Stoker Medalof Cultural Achievement by the University Philosophical Society at Trinity College, Dublin.
